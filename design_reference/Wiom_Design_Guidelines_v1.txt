1.1 WHY A DESIGN SYSTEM EXISTS

As an early-stage startup operating in a dynamic environment, maintaining consistency across products and features is challenging. A Design System serves as:

- Single source of truth
- Alignment mechanism between design & development
- Philosophy carrier
- Communication standard
- Reusable component library
- Scalability framework

It ensures:

- Consistency
- Efficiency
- Scalability
- Collaboration
- Accessibility
- Documentation clarity
- Rapid prototyping
- Brand alignment
- Improved code quality
- Knowledge sharing
- Promotion of design philosophy

Challenges (Cons):

- Upfront investment
- Maintenance overhead
- Risk of overengineering
- Resistance to change
- Learning curve
- Flexibility constraints

SECTION 2: UX WRITING PLAYBOOK (COMPLETE)
====================================================================

2.1 HINDI FIRST PHILOSOPHY

“हिंदी ही हम हैं”

Wiom writes in Hindi first because:

- Majority of households understand Hindi better
- Emotional relatability increases trust
- Cultural connection strengthens brand

Hindi is subjective and regionally varied.

Terminology Validation:

- Compare spellings via Google Trends
- Use data-backed decision making
- “Data never lies” principle

2.2 CORE COPY CHARACTERISTICS

All content must be:

1. Easy to understand
2. Properly communicate message
3. Casual and conversational

2.3 NO NEGATIVE WORDS PRINCIPLE

Wiom radiates positivity.

Avoid negative connotations unless strategically necessary.

Reasons:

1. Positive user perception
2. Increased engagement
3. Reduced frustration
4. Encourages exploration
5. Builds positive brand image

2.4 AUDIENCE BEFORE CONTENT

Before writing:

- Who is the audience?
- What do they need?
- What is our goal?

Checklist:

1. Understand audience
2. Define objective
3. Use correct tone
4. Highlight benefits
5. Keep concise
6. Include CTA

2.5 CTA FRAMEWORK

CTAs must:

- Clearly inform next action
- Reduce uncertainty
- Prepare user mentally

Rules:

- 3 words ideal
- 4–5 words acceptable
- Clarity
- Urgency
- Value communication

Casing:

- 1–3 words → Title Case
- 4+ words → Sentence case

2.6 F-PATTERN PRINCIPLE

User scanning behavior:

- Start top-left
- Horizontal scan
- Drop down
- Repeat

Design Implication:

- Critical info top-left
- Strong hierarchy
- Scannable structure

2.7 STAY CASUAL, NOT INFORMAL

Tone:

- Friendly
- Warm
- Respectful

Avoid:

- Harshness
- Over-familiarity
- Commanding tone

2.8 FAMILY BRAND POSITIONING

Content must:

- Be understandable by all family members
- Cater to decision-makers
- Feel inclusive

2.9 BENEFIT FIRST, ACTION LATER

Sequence:

1. Show benefit
2. Then prompt action

Why:

- Grabs attention
- Increases intent
- Motivates engagement

2.10 KEEP IT SHORT & SIMPLE

Users spend ~10 seconds per screen.

- Remove fluff
- Avoid overload
- Deliver exact needed info

2.11 USER PERSONALIZATION

- Address user directly
- Make interaction feel two-way
- Build emotional bond

2.12 POP-UP & ERROR STATES

Must:

- Be direct
- Be clear
- Avoid blame tone
- Provide next action

Purpose:

- Validate functionality
- Confirm usability
- Test real-world interaction

Process:

- Provide feedback
- Fix issues pre-launch

3.2 USER-CENTRIC INSIGHT FRAMEWORK

- Identify daily pain points
- Understand mental models
- Evaluate impact of each design change

3.3 INFORMATION HIERARCHY

Structure:

Headline → Support → Benefit → CTA

3.4 CONFIDENCE BUILDING

Ensure:

- Predictable flows
- Step-by-step guidance
- Clear expectations
